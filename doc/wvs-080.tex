\vspace{-20pt}

We calculate $T_\text{scat}$ by propagating a ray from space through a sample
atmosphere to a specified point $s$.  The points on that ray are called $r$ in
the sequel.

At $s$ we have the phase function

\begin{equation*}
P_s(\theta) = \frac{\lambda^2}{2 \pi \beta_{c\_s}}
  \int_0^\infty n(r)\, p_0(\theta,r)\, \text{d} r\,,
\end{equation*}

$\frac{\partial P_s(\theta)}{\partial x_s}$, radiance $T_{b_s}$ resulting from
radiative transfer along the ray to $s$, $\frac{\partial T_{b_s}}{\partial x_s}$,
and $\frac{\partial T_{b_s}}{\partial x_r}$, where $\theta$ is the angle between
the incident ray and the scattered ray that originates at $s$,  $x$ is
temperature, IWC, or a mixing ratio.

$T_\text{scat}$ at $s$ is defined as

\begin{equation*}
T_{\text{scat}_s} =
 \frac12 \int_{-\pi}^\pi P_s(\theta) T_{b_s}(\theta) \sin\theta
  \, \text{d}\theta\,.
\end{equation*}

From this

\begin{equation*}
\frac{\partial T_{\text{scat}_s}}{\partial x_s} =
 \frac12 \int_{-\pi}^\pi
  \left( \frac{\partial P_s(\theta)}{\partial x_s} T_{b_s}(\theta) +
         P_s(\theta) \frac{\partial T_{b_s}(\theta)}{\partial x_s} \right)
  \sin\theta \, \text{d} \theta
\end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*}
\frac{\partial T_{\text{scat}_s}}{\partial x_r} =
 \frac12 \int_{-\pi}^\pi 
  \left( \frac{\partial P_s(\theta)}{\partial x_r} T_{b_s}(\theta) +
          P_s(\theta) \frac{\partial T_{b_s}(\theta)}{\partial x_r} \right)
  \sin\theta \, \text{d} \theta =
 \frac12 \int_{-\pi}^\pi P_s(\theta) \frac{\partial T_{b_s}(\theta)}{\partial x_r}
  \sin\theta \, \text{d} \theta
\end{equation*}

since $P_s(\theta)$ does not depend upon $x_r$.
