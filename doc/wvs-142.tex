\vspace{-20pt}

Given a point $\mathbf{P}$ and a line $\mathcal{L}(s) = \mathbf{C} + s\,
\mathbf{U}$, where $\mathbf{C}$ is a point on the line and $\mathbf{U}$ is
an unit vector along the line, find the value $\hat{s}$ such that the
point $\mathbf{N}(\hat{s}) = \mathbf{C} + \hat{s}\, \mathbf{U}$ is the
point on the line that is nearest to $\mathbf{P}$.

\vspace*{0.1in}

\includegraphics{wvs-142-1}

The point $\mathbf{N}(\hat{s})$ on $\mathcal{L}(s)$ that is nearest to
$\mathbf{P}$ is on a line that is perpendicular to $\mathcal{L}(s)$ and
that passes through $\mathbf{P}$.

From the diagram, it is clear that the distance $\hat{s}$ along
$\mathcal{L}(s)$ from $\mathbf{C}$ to $\mathbf{N}(\hat{s})$ is the length
of the projection of $\mathbf{P}-\mathbf{C}$ onto $\mathcal{L}(s)$, or
$\hat{s} = (\mathbf{P} - \mathbf{C}) \cdot \mathbf{U}$.  Therefore the
vector to the point $\mathbf{N}(\hat{s}) = \mathbf{C} + \hat{s}\,
\mathbf{U}$.  The distance from $\mathbf{N}(\hat{s})$ to $\mathbf{P}$ is
$|\mathbf{P} - \mathbf{N}(\hat{s})|$.
